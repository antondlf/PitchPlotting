Hello and welcome to our study! We are interested in intonation, which is basically the melody of speech. Second-language learners often find it difficult to acquire the intonation patterns of their target language. In phase one of the study, English-speaking learners of Italian did an online training aimed at helping them improve their intonation/prosody in Italian. The learners read some sentences out loud, did the training, and then read some new sentences out loud. Your task is to listen to pairs of recordings and guess which one is from after the training. In other words, which recording sounds better / more Italian? As much as possible, please try to judge the recordings based on their intonation and not on the pronunciation of the words. Note it is often really hard to tell which recording is better, and that's totally fine! Just go with your instinct, and if you really have no idea, just guess.
