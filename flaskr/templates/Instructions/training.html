Once you’re done with the initial recordings, you will be automatically taken to the training section of the module, which features the following interface:

{% if condition == 'a' %}

In this interface you will be shown a plot of the intonation with the words aligned at their corresponding intonational events. Examine this plot and try to pronounce the sentence for yourself. Then, you should press the “Play Recording” button, which will play a recording of a native speaker pronouncing the sentence. Press “Record”, wait for the “Go!” sign, and try to imitate the native speaker’s production. Once you press “Stop”, you will be redirected to another page (this may take a few seconds to process).

This page will display a plot of your intonation against the native speaker, so you can compare both traces and see what you did right and what you did wrong. The example here is from a learner who hasn't quite hit the right intonation pattern but is almost there. You can see that the second syllable has quite a different intonation when compared to the native speaker, but the rest of the sentence aligns. You can evaluate your own intonation visually like this, and you also have the opportunity to listen to the native speaker again to get a better idea of where you went wrong by listening back to yourself.

{% elif condition == 'b' %}

In this interface you will be asked to listen to a native speaker, which you can do by pressing “Play Recording”. You should try to pronounce the sentence, listen to the native speaker, and then record yourself (using the “Record” button) imitating the native speaker’s intonation.

You will then be redirected to another page where you will be able to listen to the native speaker once more before moving on to the next sentence.
